Why Marriage Registration Is Mandatory

Marriage registration is not merely a procedural formality; it is a legally valid written proof of marriage (Marriage Certificate). In modern legal and administrative systems, this certificate plays a vital role in establishing the marital relationship between husband and wife.

A marriage certificate is required for various official and legal purposes such as passport and visa applications, banking and insurance matters, government schemes, proof of spousal relationship, court cases, property rights and other legal proceedings.

Earlier in India, marriages‚Äîespecially under Hindu marriage customs‚Äîwere solemnized through religious ceremonies. However, there was no formal government record to officially establish that a particular man is the lawful husband of a woman or that a woman is the legally wedded wife of a man.

To address this gap, the Hindu Marriage Registration Act was introduced, which made it possible to officially register Hindu marriages and create a valid government record of marriage.

Over time, legislation was enacted to provide for marriage registration across all religions and communities, ensuring uniform legal recognition of marriages.

At present, marriage registration has been made compulsory for all religions, ensuring legal certainty and protection of spousal rights.
