NRI Property & Legal Compliance – FAQs

Is TDS applicable on sale of property by NRI in India?

Yes. TDS is mandatory on property sale by NRIs under the Income Tax Act. The rate depends on whether the capital gain is long-term or short-term. Proper calculation, TDS deduction, deposit and Form 26QB compliance are essential to avoid penalties.

Do NRIs need GPA or SPA for property transactions in India?

Yes. NRIs commonly execute General Power of Attorney (GPA) or Special Power of Attorney (SPA) to authorise property sale, purchase or registration in India. The POA must be executed abroad and adjudicated or registered in India to become legally valid.

Is adjudication or registration of Power of Attorney mandatory?

Yes. A Power of Attorney executed outside India must be adjudicated or stamped in India within the prescribed time. In certain transactions, registration of GPA or SPA is mandatory to ensure full legal validity and acceptance by authorities.

Can NRIs obtain succession certificate, mutation and title transfer in India?

Yes. NRIs can apply for succession certificate, legal heir certificate, mutation of property records and transfer of title through authorised legal representatives in India without personal presence, subject to proper documentation and court procedures.

Do you provide complete end-to-end disposal of NRI property in India?

Yes. We provide complete end-to-end NRI property services including legal due diligence, documentation, drafting, registration, tax compliance, mutation, succession matters and lawful disposal of property in Ghaziabad and Delhi NCR.

Can an NRI repatriate the entire sale proceeds of a property in India to their foreign bank account?

Yes. According to RBI guidelines under FEMA, an NRI can repatriate up to $1 Million USD per financial year from India to their overseas bank account. This process requires compliance with Form 15CA and Form 15CB certifications. Our experienced team of chartered accountants handles the entire documentation and banking compliance smoothly.

Are the rules for selling property in India different for an OCI Cardholder and an NRI?

Yes. For residential and commercial properties, the rules are largely identical for both NRIs and OCI cardholders. However, the regulations regarding the sale, transfer, or inheritance of agricultural land, plantation property, or farmhouses are strictly monitored and restricted. Comprehensive legal due diligence is highly recommended in such cases to ensure complete regulatory compliance.

If a Power of Attorney (POA) is executed and signed abroad, how does it become legally valid in India?

Yes. A Power of Attorney executed outside India must be brought to India and adjudicated (stamped and verified) within 3 months of its receipt in India. This process is conducted at the office of the concerned District Magistrate or Sub-Registrar (such as in Ghaziabad, Indirapuram, or Delhi NCR) to make it legally enforceable and valid for property registration.

Can an NRI apply for a Lower TDS Certificate for property sale in India?

Yes. NRIs can significantly reduce their tax withholding by applying for a Lower TDS Certificate under Section 197 of the Income Tax Act. Instead of the standard 20-23% deduction on the total sale value, the TDS will only be calculated on your actual capital gains (profits). This prevents your hard-earned funds from being unnecessarily blocked with the income tax department.

Are PAN and TAN numbers mandatory for NRI property transactions?

Yes.specific tax identity numbers are essential. The NRI seller must possess a valid PAN Card to execute the sale and claim tax benefits. Simultaneously, the buyer requires a TAN (Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number) or must submit Form 26QB to deduct and deposit the TDS into the government account. Our legal firm manages these financial compliances end-to-end for both parties.
