Our Key Legal Drafting Services:

  • Sale Deed (बिक्री विलेख): Legal document for complete transfer of property ownership. Essential for lawful sale and purchase. Advocate drafting ensures accurate clauses and risk-free transfer. Proper registration is mandatory for legal validity.
  • Gift Deed (गिफ्ट डीड): Transfers property without monetary consideration. Prevents future family and ownership disputes. Legal drafting avoids errors that may invalidate the gift. Registered Gift Deed is legally enforceable.
  • Will / Vasiyat (वसीयत): Specifies distribution of assets after death. Helps avoid family disputes and litigation. Advocate drafting ensures compliance with legal requirements. Registration increases authenticity and prevents misuse.
  • Power of Attorney (POA): Authorizes another person to act on your behalf. Useful for property, banking, and legal matters. Professional drafting prevents misuse of authority. Registered POA is accepted by government departments.
  • Agreement to Sell: Confirms the intent to sell a property before final sale. Defines terms and conditions to avoid disputes. Advocate drafting ensures enforceable and risk-free agreements. Registration provides legal protection to both parties.
  • Rent Deed: Defines landlord and tenant rights. Clarifies rent, terms, and duration. UP government relief: Annual rent up to ₹10 lakh can be registered on ₹4500 stamp paper. Proper drafting ensures enforceability.
  • Leave & License Agreement: Temporary property occupation agreement. Prevents disputes over duration, rent, or property usage. Advocate drafting ensures compliance with UP government rules. Registration provides legal recognition and protection.
  • Relinquishment Deed: Used when a co-owner gives up rights in a property. Ensures smooth legal transfer and prevents future claims. Proper drafting clarifies rights and obligations. Registered deed ensures legal enforceability.
  • Settlement Deed: Documents settlement between parties regarding property or financial matters. Prevents future conflicts. Legal drafting ensures clarity and enforceability. Registration provides full legal recognition.
  • Partnership Deed: Defines rights, responsibilities, and profit sharing among partners. Prevents disputes and misunderstandings. Advocate drafting ensures compliance with partnership laws. Registered deed strengthens legal enforceability.
  • MOU (Memorandum of Understanding): Preliminary agreement outlining intentions. Clarifies roles, responsibilities, and terms before final agreement. Drafting by legal experts ensures enforceability. Serves as evidence in case of future disputes.
  • Legal Notice / Reply: Professional legal communication for claims, demands, or disputes. Drafting ensures correct legal language and compliance. Prevents invalid or rejected notices. Properly drafted notices strengthen legal position.
  • Assignment Deed: Transfers property, contractual, or intellectual property rights. Drafting ensures precise terms and protections. Registered deed is legally recognized. Prevents future disputes over ownership or rights.
  • Service Agreement: Defines terms of employment, consultancy, or services. Prevents misunderstandings between parties. Legal drafting ensures enforceability and compliance. Registration enhances legal validity.

    • Legal Heir Services: Legal Succession and Property Transfer

    To transfer the movable and immovable property of a deceased person to the rightful heirs, a Legal Heir Certificate or Succession Certificate is mandatory. This process involves technical legal nuances, making the guidance of an experienced Lawyer or Advocate essential.

    Why is a Legal Heir Certificate Necessary?

    • To claim the deceased's bank accounts and Fixed Deposits (FD).
    • For stock market investments and insurance claims.
    • To process property mutation (Dakhil-Kharij) in the Ghaziabad Development Authority (GDA) or Municipal Corporation.
    • To receive pension and other government benefits.

    Process and Documentation:

    • Death Certificate of the deceased.
    • Identity cards of all legal heirs (Aadhaar Card/Voter ID).
    • Family Tree (Vanshavali).
    • Details of the assets/property.

    Heirs are determined according to the Hindu Succession Act or relevant personal laws.
